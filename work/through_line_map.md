# Through-Line: Portfolio Content Map

## One-line claim

I turn messy search and content data into clear ranking systems that help teams focus on the pages most worth fixing.

## Content map

### 1. Home / Landing page

Sections in order:

1. Hero statement with the one-line claim.
2. Short intro explaining the problem space: too much content, too little time, and the need for prioritization.
3. Featured work card for the Refresh Opportunity Scorer.
4. CTA: "Let’s talk about your content prioritization problem."

Primary case: Refresh Opportunity Scorer

### 2. Case study page: Refresh Opportunity Scorer

Sections in order:

1. Problem: content decay and noisy manual triage.
2. My decision: frame it as a ranking problem, not just a rule-based cleanup.
3. Approach: transparent baseline, feature window, honest modeling, and leakage avoidance.
4. Outcome: better ranking quality and a practical priority queue.
5. CTA: "See how I would approach your workflow."

Primary case: Refresh Opportunity Scorer

### 3. About page

Sections in order:

1. Short bio focused on applied search intelligence and practical ML.
2. Technical stack and working style.
3. CTA: "Get in touch about a project or conversation."

Primary case: none; this page supports trust and connection.

### 4. Contact page

Sections in order:

1. Brief invitation to connect.
2. Email or form CTA.
3. Optional note on the kind of problems I like to solve.

CTA: "Email me to start a conversation."

- Home: "Let’s talk about your content prioritization problem."
